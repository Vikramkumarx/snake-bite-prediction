üêç Do's and Don'ts When Encountering Snakes

‚úÖDO's

  • Keep a safe distance (at least 6 feet)
  • Stay calm and move slowly away
  • Watch where you step in snake habitat
  • Wear boots and long pants in snake areas
  • Use a flashlight at night
  • Keep your yard clean and trimmed
  • Educate yourself about local snakes
  • Take a photo from a safe distance

‚ùåDON'Ts

  • Don't try to catch or kill the snake
  • Don't provoke or corner the snake
  • Don't put your hands in holes or crevices
  • Don't step over logs without checking
  • Don't handle dead snakes (can still bite)
  • Don't assume a snake is harmless
  • Don't panic or make sudden movements
  • Don't try to identify by getting close

üöë First Aid for Snake Bites

‚ö†Ô∏èIMPORTANT: These are general guidelines. Always seek immediate professional medical help for any snake bite!

Immediate Steps:

1. Call Emergency Services (911)

Time is critical. Call for help immediately, even if you're not sure if the snake was venomous.

2. Keep the Victim Calm

Panic increases heart rate and spreads venom faster. Keep the person still and reassured.

3. Position the Victim

Keep the bitten area below heart level if possible. Have the person lie down.

4. Remove Tight Items

Take off rings, watches, tight clothing near the bite before swelling starts.

5. Clean the Wound

Gently wash with soap and water if available. Cover with a clean, dry dressing.

6. Immobilize the Limb

Use a splint to keep the bitten limb still. Minimize movement.

7. Note the Time

Record when the bite occurred. This helps medical professionals.

8. Identify the Snake (Safely)

Take a photo from a safe distance or note its appearance. Don't try to catch it!

‚ùå What NOT to Do:

  • DON'T apply ice or cold packs
  • DON'T use a tourniquet
  • DON'T cut the wound
  • DON'T try to suck out the venom
  • DON'T apply electric shock
  • DON'T give the victim alcohol or caffeine
  • DON'T let the victim walk or run
  • DON'T wait to see if symptoms develop

üîç Snake Identification Tips

Visual Characteristics to Note:

  • Head Shape: Triangular (venomous) vs. Rounded (often harmless)
  • Eyes: Elliptical pupils (venomous) vs. Round pupils (harmless)
  • Color Patterns: Bands, stripes, or solid colors
  • Size: Length and thickness of the snake
  • Tail: Rattles (rattlesnakes) or smooth
‚ö†Ô∏èRemember: These are general guidelines. Some harmless snakes mimic venomous ones. Never rely solely on these features. When in doubt, treat all snakes as potentially dangerous!

Common Venomous Snake Patterns in North America:

  • Coral Snakes: "Red on yellow, kill a fellow; Red on black, friend of Jack"
  • Pit Vipers: Triangular heads, heat-sensing pits between eyes and nostrils
  • Rattlesnakes: Distinctive rattle at the end of tail
  • Cottonmouths: White mouth interior, semi-aquatic

üè° Preventing Snake Encounters

Around Your Home:

  • Keep grass short and remove brush piles
  • Seal cracks and gaps in foundations
  • Remove rodent food sources (birdseed, pet food)
  • Store firewood away from the house
  • Install snake-proof fencing if needed
  • Keep garage doors closed
  • Remove standing water

While Hiking or Outdoors:

  • Stay on designated trails
  • Wear sturdy boots and long pants
  • Use a walking stick to probe ahead
  • Watch where you sit or place your hands
  • Be extra cautious near water and rocks
  • Avoid hiking at dawn, dusk, or night
  • Make noise while walking to alert snakes
